Coconut Water Requirements in Sri Lanka

← Back to Home

Overview

This research project investigates the water requirements for coconut cultivation in Sri Lanka, addressing the current coconut shortage through scientific analysis of crop water demand versus available supply.

The study focuses on:

  • Calculating crop water requirements using FAO-56 Penman-Monteith method
  • Analyzing water deficit across different climatic zones
  • Comparing irrigation methods for coconut cultivation
  • Validating results with meteorological data

Key Findings

  • Water Deficit Analysis: Significant water deficits were identified in key coconut-growing districts, particularly during dry seasons.
  • Climatic Variations: ET₀ (Reference Evapotranspiration) showed considerable variation across Sri Lanka's climatic zones, ranging from 4-6 mm/day in wet zones to 5-7 mm/day in dry zones.
  • Supply vs Requirement: Current water supply meets only 60-75% of coconut water requirements in major production areas during peak demand periods.

Methodology

Data Collection and Processing

The study utilized Google Earth Engine for comprehensive data extraction and analysis (1990-2024):

  • Precipitation Data: CHIRPS (Climate Hazards Group InfraRed Precipitation with Station data) daily precipitation dataset at 0.05° resolution
  • Meteorological Data: ERA5 reanalysis data for temperature, humidity, wind speed, and solar radiation
  • Validation: Ground station measurements from Sri Lanka Meteorological Department

FAO 56 Penman-Monteith Implementation

The reference evapotranspiration (ET₀) was calculated using the standardized FAO 56 Penman-Monteith equation implemented in Google Earth Engine:

  • Full implementation available at: Earth Engine Code Link
  • Equation incorporates:
    • Net radiation at crop surface (Rn)
    • Soil heat flux density (G)
    • Mean daily air temperature (T)
    • Wind speed at 2m height (u₂)
    • Actual vapor pressure (eₐ)
    • Saturation vapor pressure (eₛ)
    • Slope of vapor pressure curve (Δ)
    • Psychrometric constant (γ)
  • Daily ET₀ calculations aggregated to monthly and seasonal values
  • Crop coefficients (Kc) applied for coconut-specific water requirements

Water Balance Analysis

  • Water deficit calculated as difference between crop water requirement and effective precipitation
  • Spatial analysis conducted at district level for major coconut growing regions
  • Time series analysis for seasonal and interannual variability

Data Analysis

  • Data Validation: Strong correlation between ERA5 reanalysis data and ground station measurements (R² = 0.86-0.90 for temperature, 0.74 for precipitation).
  • Seasonal Patterns: Highest water deficits occur from June to September in the Dry Zone, coinciding with critical coconut growth stages.
  • District Variations: Puttalam, Kurunegala, and Gampaha districts show the most significant water deficits during peak demand periods.

Irrigation Methods Comparison

The study evaluated three irrigation methods for coconut cultivation:

  • Surface Irrigation: Traditional method with 50-60% efficiency, requiring 120-150 liters/tree/day
  • Drip Irrigation: Most efficient (85-90%), reducing water use to 80-100 liters/tree/day
  • Sprinkler Irrigation: 70-75% efficiency, using 100-120 liters/tree/day

Recommendations

  • Prioritize drip irrigation systems in water-scarce coconut-growing regions
  • Implement district-specific water management strategies based on deficit patterns
  • Develop early warning systems for water stress periods
  • Enhance rainwater harvesting during wet seasons to supplement dry season irrigation
